About My Hometown

Barishal, officially known as Barisal, is a major city and the administrative headquarter of both Barishal District and Barishal Division in southern Bangladesh. Located on the banks of the Kirtankhola River, this historic port city is often called the "Venice of the East" due to its intricate network of rivers, canals, and waterways.

With a history dating back to the Mughal era, Barishal has played a significant role in Bangladesh's cultural and economic development. The city is famous for its paddy fields, coconut groves, and betel leaf cultivation. The region's economy is primarily based on agriculture, fishing, and trade.

Barishal is also renowned for its educational institutions, including Barishal University, Sher-e-Bangla Medical College, and numerous historic madrasas. The city has produced many prominent intellectuals, poets, and political leaders who have contributed significantly to Bengali culture and the Bangladesh independence movement.

Taste of Barishal

Traditional Dishes

  • Kachki Macher Jhol (Small Fish Curry)
  • Shorshe Ilish (Hilsa in Mustard Sauce)
  • Chingri Malai Curry (Prawn Coconut Curry)
  • Panta Bhat (Fermented Rice)
  • Bhorta (Mashed Vegetables/Fish)

Local Specialties

  • Narkel Naru (Coconut Balls)
  • Chitol Macher Muithya (Fish Balls)
  • Koi Macher Tel Jhal (Climbing Perch in Chili Oil)
  • Shutki Bhorta (Dried Fish Mash)
  • Pitha (Traditional Rice Cakes)

Street Foods

  • Fuchka (Panipuri)
  • Jhalmuri (Spicy Puffed Rice)
  • Chotpoti (Spicy Chickpea Snack)
  • Singara (Samosa)
  • Mughlai Paratha (Stuffed Flatbread)
